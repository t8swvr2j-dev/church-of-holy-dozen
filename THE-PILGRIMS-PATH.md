## Addition Overflows

The baker gives 11z (13d) loaves when advertising a dozen. Why?

Not because 11z is sacred ‚Äî it is profane. But because the baker:
1. Begins with the sacred 10z (12d)
2. Adds one as **grace** ‚Äî insurance against imperfection, a gift beyond the minimum

This is **generosity as pilgrimage**: giving beyond the sacred minimum, stepping into profane territory as an act of love.

## The Doctrine Revealed

**Division and multiplication reveal the eternal Truth of Dozen** ‚Äî the unchanging structure of Divine Divisibility. These operations show us what *is*.

The baker's dozen is not heresy. It is **pilgrimage with provisions** ‚Äî carrying one extra to ensure the sacred number survives the journey.

## Reconciling the Realms

Some have asked: if division reveals truth, why does the Faith not condemn addition?

Because **addition and subtraction serve division**. We add and subtract *in order to reach* numbers that divide well. The child learning arithmetic adds 4 + 4 + 4 and discovers: "This equals 10z!" Through addition, the structure of multiplication is revealed.

The realms are not opposed. They are **complementary**:

|-------|---------|-------------|
| Being (√ó, √∑) | Reveals structure | Shows the destination |
| Becoming (+, ‚àí) | Enables movement | Walks the path |

You cannot reach the sacred by multiplication alone ‚Äî you must first arrive at a number worth multiplying. You cannot reveal divisibility by subtraction alone ‚Äî you must first possess something to divide.

**Being and Becoming. Structure and Journey. Destination and Path.**

Both are holy.
